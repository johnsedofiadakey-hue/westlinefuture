# Your Project Journey with Westline Future
### A Complete Walkthrough — From First Conversation to Final Handover

---

> This document walks you through every step of your custom furniture and interior project with Westline Future. Keep it handy. At each stage, you'll know exactly what we're doing, what you need to do, and what to expect next.

---

## Before We Begin — Your Client Portal

Everything about your project lives in one place: **your Westline Future Client Portal**.

**How to access it:**
- Visit your personal portal link (shared by your account manager via WhatsApp or SMS)
- Enter your registered phone number
- You'll receive a one-time code via SMS — enter it to sign in
- No password to remember. The code expires after a few minutes, so use it immediately

**What's inside your portal:**

| Tab | What it's for |
|---|---|
| **Overview** | Your full project roadmap — see every stage, what's active, and what's next |
| **Financials** | View invoices, see amounts due, and pay securely online |
| **Designs** | Review and approve your 3D renderings |
| **Documents** | Access your contract, quotation, scope document, and handover certificate |
| **Messages** | Chat directly with your project manager |

> **Tip:** Add the portal to your phone's home screen for instant access. It works like an app — your project manager can show you how.

---

## Payment Structure — How It Works

Your project is billed in three milestone payments tied to progress:

| Milestone | When It's Due | Amount |
|---|---|---|
| **Initial Deposit** | On quotation approval and contract signing | **60%** of project total |
| **Production Balance** | When manufacturing is complete, before shipping | **30%** of project total |
| **Final Arrival Balance** | When goods arrive in Ghana | **10%** of project total |

> **Note:** Installation is a separate service billed as an add-on. If you have requested full-service delivery with installation, an installation invoice will be issued at Stage 5 when your goods arrive.

All payments are made securely through your portal using mobile money or card.

## The 8 Project Stages

---

### Stage 1 — Onboarding, Payment & Site Survey
**⏱ Estimated duration: ~10 days**

This is where your project officially begins.

#### What Westline does:
- Creates your project and client account
- Sets up your personal portal
- Assigns your dedicated project manager
- Assigns the technical survey team for your site visit

#### What you need to do:

**1. Access your portal**
Your project manager will send you the portal link via WhatsApp. Log in with your phone number and the SMS code.

**2. Pay the rendering fee**
The 3D rendering is a separate, one-time fee — it's not part of your project total. Go to the **Financials** tab in your portal and pay this fee to unlock the design stage. Without this payment, we cannot begin your 3D design.

**3. Confirm your site survey appointment**
Your project manager will contact you directly to schedule a technical site visit. Please ensure:
- Someone with full access to the space is available on the agreed date and time
- All areas to be covered by the project are accessible (rooms, storage areas, alcoves)
- Existing furniture that is staying is in place so dimensions can be planned around it

**4. During the site visit — what our team will do:**
- Take precise measurements of every surface, wall, ceiling height, and floor area
- Document existing fittings, electrical points, plumbing, and structural features
- Take reference photographs of the space
- Note any obstacles, access constraints, or special requirements you raise

**Documents at this stage:**
- None required from you
- Your project manager may ask for any existing floor plans or architectural drawings if you have them (not mandatory)

**Stage complete when:**
✅ Rendering fee paid
✅ Site survey completed and signed off by the technical team
✅ Measurements and photos uploaded to your project

---

### Stage 2 — 3D Rendering Review
**⏱ Estimated duration: ~10 days**

Our design team works from the site measurements to produce a detailed 3D visualisation of your finished space.

#### What Westline does:
- Builds the 3D model from your site measurements and brief
- Prepares the first rendering version for your review
- Uploads it to your portal under the **Designs** tab
- Notifies you when it's ready

#### What you need to do:

**1. Open the Designs tab**
Your portal will show an "Action Required" banner directing you here when your rendering is ready.

**2. Review carefully**
Check the following in the rendering:
- Layout and placement of all furniture pieces
- Dimensions and proportions relative to the room
- Materials, colours, and finishes shown
- Any built-in features (wardrobes, shelving, cabinetry etc.)
- Lighting considerations if applicable

**3. Request revisions if needed**
If anything is not right — a colour you want changed, a dimension that doesn't feel right, a piece you want repositioned — click **Request Changes** and describe exactly what you'd like adjusted. Be as specific as possible. Our designers will revise and re-upload.

> You may request revisions as many times as needed within the agreed revision policy discussed with your project manager.

**4. Approve the final version**
When you are satisfied with the design, click **Approve Design**. This is a critical step — it confirms you have reviewed and agreed to the layout and specifications that will go into production. Changes after approval may not be possible.

**Documents at this stage:**
- No uploads required from you
- Westline uploads the 3D rendering file(s) to your Designs tab

**Stage complete when:**
✅ You have approved the final 3D rendering in your portal

### Stage 3 — Commercial Approval & Production Authority
**⏱ Estimated duration: ~14 days**

This is the commercial gateway. Four things must happen in sequence before production can begin.

#### What Westline does:
- Prepares a detailed project quotation based on the approved design
- Issues the quotation to your portal for review
- Prepares the project contract once the quotation is agreed
- Prepares the final scope and deliverables document once payment is verified

#### What you need to do — in order:

---

**Step 1: Review and Approve the Quotation**

Go to the **Documents** tab (or click the "Review Quotation" prompt on your Overview). Your quotation will show:

- A full scope summary — exactly what is included
- What is **included** in the price: all materials, sourcing, procurement, production, quality control, shipping to Ghana, customs clearance, and port charges
- What is **excluded**: on-site installation (billed separately), and any additions or changes requested after approval
- The full payment schedule (60% / 30% / 10%) with amounts in GHS

You have three options:
- **Approve** — you accept the quoted amount and scope. This locks in the price and triggers contract signing.
- **Request Changes** — you want something adjusted in the scope or you have a question. Your project manager will respond and may issue a revised quotation.
- **Counter Offer** — you want to propose a different price. Enter your counter figure and a note. Your project manager will review and respond.

> Once you approve, the quoted amount is agreed and fixed. Any additions to scope after this point are billed as separate add-on invoices.

---

**Step 2: Sign the Contract**

After quotation approval, your project contract appears in your portal. Read it carefully — it covers:
- The agreed project scope and deliverables
- The payment schedule
- Production and delivery timelines
- Warranty terms
- Rights and responsibilities of both parties

To sign: click **Sign Contract**. You will receive a one-time SMS code to your registered number. Enter it to apply your electronic signature. This is legally binding.

> Keep a copy — go to the **Documents** tab at any time to download your signed contract as a PDF.

---

**Step 3: Pay the 60% Initial Deposit**

After signing, your first invoice becomes active in the **Financials** tab.

- **Amount:** 60% of the agreed project total
- **How to pay:** Mobile money (MTN, Telecel, AirtelTigo) or card — directly in your portal
- **What it unlocks:** Payment verification triggers procurement and production

> Please pay promptly. Production cannot begin until the deposit is verified. Bank transfers take longer to verify than mobile money.

---

**Step 4: Approve the Final Scope & Deliverables Document**

Once your deposit is verified, your project manager will upload a final deliverables document to your portal. This document confirms in writing every item that will be produced and delivered — quantities, dimensions, finishes, and references back to the approved 3D design.

Review it carefully and click **Authorise Production**. This is your final confirmation that the production brief is correct before the factory begins work.

> This is your last opportunity to raise any discrepancy between what was quoted and what is listed. Once authorised, the production team begins fabrication immediately.

**Documents at this stage:**
- **Quotation** — issued by Westline, available in your Documents tab
- **Project Contract** — issued by Westline, signed electronically by you
- **Final Scope & Deliverables** — issued by Westline, authorised by you

**Stage complete when:**
✅ Quotation approved
✅ Contract signed
✅ 60% deposit paid and verified
✅ Final scope & deliverables authorised

### Stage 4 — Procurement & Production
**⏱ Estimated duration: ~30 days**

This is the longest stage. Your custom furniture is being built.

#### What Westline does:
- Procures all specified raw materials (wood, hardware, fabrics, finishes, fittings)
- Schedules production at our manufacturing facility
- Custom cuts, processes, and fabricates every component to the agreed specifications
- Runs internal quality control inspection — dimensions, finishes, assembly, and function are all checked
- Packs components securely for international shipment

#### What you need to do:
**Nothing is required from you at this stage.** Your portal will show production is active. Your project manager may send you progress photos via WhatsApp as milestones are reached.

If you have any questions or concerns during this period, use the **Messages** tab in your portal.

**Documents at this stage:**
- None required from you

**Stage complete when:**
✅ Production complete
✅ Quality control passed
✅ All components packed and ready for dispatch

---

### Stage 5 — Shipping & Delivery
**⏱ Estimated duration: ~35 days**

Your furniture is packed and on its way to Ghana.

#### What Westline does:
- Arranges international freight from the production facility
- Publishes shipping details to your portal: vessel name, container number, bill of lading, and estimated arrival date
- Manages customs clearance and port handling (all duties and clearance costs are included in your project price)
- Confirms goods arrival in Ghana
- Issues your **30% Production Balance** invoice (active on arrival)
- Prepares a separate installation add-on invoice if you have requested installation

#### What you need to do:

**Track your shipment**
Your portal Overview will show live shipping information once goods are dispatched. You can see the vessel, container, and ETA at all times.

**Pay the 30% Production Balance**
When goods arrive in Ghana, your second invoice activates in the **Financials** tab.

- **Amount:** 30% of the agreed project total
- **Why now:** This confirms receipt of goods in-country before delivery to site
- **How to pay:** Same as before — mobile money or card in your portal

**If you have requested installation — Pay the Installation Invoice**
The installation service is billed separately at this point. Your project manager will issue the installation invoice and it will appear in your Financials tab. This must be paid before our installation crew will be dispatched to your site.

**Documents at this stage:**
- **Shipping manifest** — uploaded by Westline to your Documents tab (container and item list)
- No documents required from you

**Stage complete when:**
✅ Goods arrived in Ghana confirmed
✅ 30% Production Balance paid and verified
✅ Installation invoice paid (if applicable)

---

### Stage 6 — Installation
**⏱ Estimated duration: ~7 days**
*(Full-service projects only)*

Our technical crew is on-site bringing everything together.

#### What Westline does:
- Assigns the installation crew to your site
- Prepares and secures the site before work begins
- Assembles and fits all furniture components in the positions shown in the approved 3D design
- Finishes surfaces, installs hardware, and aligns all components precisely
- Uploads daily progress photos to your portal
- Conducts an internal completion check before handing over to the client inspection stage

#### What you need to do:
- Ensure the site is clear and accessible each day the crew is scheduled to work
- Do not move or alter work in progress between installation sessions
- If you spot anything that needs adjusting while the crew is on-site, raise it with the team lead immediately — it is far easier to resolve during installation than after

**Documents at this stage:**
- Daily installation photos uploaded by the crew (viewable in your portal Documents tab)
- No documents required from you

**Stage complete when:**
✅ Full installation completed
✅ Internal completion check passed
✅ Site cleared of all tools and packaging

---

### Stage 7 — Inspection & Sign-off
**⏱ Estimated duration: ~3 days**

This is your formal acceptance of the completed work.

#### What Westline does:
- Notifies you that installation is complete and ready for inspection
- Your project manager may walk through the space with you in person

#### What you need to do:

**Go through the inspection checklist in your portal** (Overview tab → "Open Inspection Checklist", or the Approvals tab directly).

You will tick off each item:

| # | Inspection Item |
|---|---|
| 1 | Workmanship quality meets expectations |
| 2 | All items match the agreed scope and specifications |
| 3 | All surfaces are clean, finished, and undamaged |
| 4 | Hardware, fittings, and mechanisms are functional |
| 5 | Dimensions and placement are as agreed |
| 6 | No visible defects, scratches, or damage observed |
| 7 | Site is clear and free of installation debris |

You may also add an optional note to record any observations.

**All 7 items must be ticked to sign off.**

If any item cannot be ticked because something is not right, **do not sign off yet.** Contact your project manager immediately via the Messages tab. Minor issues will be resolved by the crew before sign-off is requested again.

Once all items are ticked, click **Sign Off — I Accept the Completed Work.** This is permanent. It confirms your acceptance and advances the project to handover.

**Documents at this stage:**
- No uploads required from you
- Your sign-off is recorded electronically with a timestamp in the system

**Stage complete when:**
✅ All 7 inspection items ticked
✅ Client sign-off submitted

---

### Stage 8 — Handover & Closeout
**⏱ Estimated duration: ~1 day**

Your project is complete. This is the official close.

#### What Westline does:
- Issues your **Handover Certificate** — available in your Documents tab
- Activates your warranty (terms in your contract)
- Closes the project file

#### What you need to do:

**Submit your feedback in your portal** (Overview tab → "Complete Handover & Feedback", or the Approvals tab directly).

You'll be asked to:
1. **Rate your overall experience** — 1 to 5 stars
2. **Write a short review** — tell us about the quality, communication, delivery, and anything that stood out
3. **Optional:** Tick the consent box if you are happy for us to use your review as a testimonial on our website or marketing materials

Click **Submit Feedback & Complete Project.**

That's it — your project is officially concluded.

**Documents at this stage:**
- **Handover Certificate** — issued by Westline, available in your Documents tab
- **Warranty Card** — terms detailed in your contract

**Stage complete when:**
✅ Feedback submitted
✅ Project marked complete

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I make changes after approving the quotation?**
Yes, but changes after quotation approval are treated as add-ons and billed separately at the applicable rate. They may also affect the production timeline. Raise change requests early — once production has begun, some changes may not be possible.

**What if I am not happy with the rendering?**
Request as many revisions as you need before approving. You are under no obligation to approve a rendering that does not reflect your vision. Once you approve, design changes become add-on scope.

**What is included in the project price?**
All materials, sourcing, production, quality control, international shipping, customs clearance, and port charges. Installation is excluded and quoted separately.

**What does the warranty cover?**
Your warranty terms are in your signed contract. Generally, Westline Future covers manufacturing defects and structural integrity for the period stated. Wear and tear, misuse, and damage caused by the client or third parties are excluded.

**What if goods are damaged during shipping?**
Westline Future handles all logistics including insurance. If goods arrive damaged, we manage the claim and replacement at no additional cost to you. You do not need to deal with the freight company directly.

**When does the 10% balance become due?**
When your goods physically arrive in Ghana and Westline Future confirms receipt. This happens simultaneously with the 30% Production Balance — both are triggered by the same arrival event. They may appear as two separate invoices in your portal.

**Can I visit the factory during production?**
Factory visits can be arranged by request and are subject to scheduling. Speak to your project manager if you would like to see production in progress.
